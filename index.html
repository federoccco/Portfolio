My Journey

It is indeed true that I slept for about 10 years of my life, but what did I do while I was awake? My path was anything but linear. Growing up I developed several temporary passions which used to die after few months/years. I have been constantly working part-time while studying for the passion of the moment.

I wasn't born with a dream. I wish I had one. Things would have been much easier. You know your path and you just do your best to follow it. I had to figure it out. "What do I like to do?" This is the question I asked every morning to myself for the past 15 years. I tried several things from sports to travel the world hoping to clear my mind and seek answers.

From elementary school to highschool I tried different sports like football, tennis and volleyball. I went to university without really thinking about it, I just believed that it was what I was supposed to do. The natural course of events. On my 3rd year, for the first time I stopped acting like a machine whose path was written by someone else and realized that maybe that wasn't truly what I wanted to do. (Spoiler: It was)
I dropped university and had some hard times. I was missing a purpose, something to achieve. I started doing some part time jobs to earn some money while I was spending the rest ot the time experimenting and studying.

I went to Japan in 2018 trying again to clear my mind and find something I didn't know I was looking for. It didn't helped to find my path, but it was the best eperienced I had in my life. Learning a language and culture so distant from what I was used to really opened my eyes. Once I got back, I tried to attend university for the second time, but after 2 years I again realized that wasn't what I wanted to do. Thanks to a friend I discovered back programming languages and started to learn it as a hobby until I realized I was learning it for so long that maybe finally I found my path.

Ironically, I closed the cycle right where it began doing the same thing I was doing when I started (my mother doesn't miss a day remembering it to me). I got passioned about data analysis and data science and here I am. Basically it's the 19 years old me, with 10 more years of self consciousness on my shoulder. I am hungry now, hungry to prove that all this time wasn't wasted, but it was what it took to make me the person I am.
